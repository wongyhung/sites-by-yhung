My Story

Hey, 👋 I’m Yhung. I never thought I’d be writing this as a web developer today, but here I am. I kind of studied Information Technology in college. By “kind of”, I mean I dropped out in the first year. Yeah, that happened. I then tried finding my calling in all sorts of work; fitness personal training, forex trading, customer support, sales, and account management- none of which stuck to me.

At some point of going in circles and having a existential crisis, I stumbled upon The Odin Project, an online course that teaches web development. I thought to myself, why not? And that was when I found my true passion in building websites. Fast forward to today, I’ve turned that passion into a career where I get to help small businesses create meaningful websites that are not just functional, but also beautiful and user-friendly!

Whether it’s building a brand-new website or revamping an old one, my goal is to elevate your brand with a strong online presence. Here’s what I bring to the table:


  • ✨ Web Dev Magic: HTML, CSS, JavaScript—I’m excited to continue growing my skill set as I take on new projects!
  • 📱 User-Friendly & Responsive Design: No one likes a confusing website. I make sure your site is a breeze to navigate.
  • ⚙️ Custom Sites: I love working with small businesses to create refined websites that actually deliver results.
  • 🔎 SEO-Friendly: I ensure your site is optimized for search engines, making it easier for customers to find you online.
  • 🤝 Ongoing Support: I’m here for you after launch, offering ongoing support to keep everything running smoothly.


I’m all about making things simple, clean, and fun. No stuffy corporate stuff here—just websites that are as unique as your business. When I’m not making websites, you can probably find me:


  • 🎮 Trying to climb out of my hard-stuck rank in League of Legends
  • 📦 Building cool stuff in Minecraft
  • 🏋️‍♂️ Pumping iron at the gym
  • 🍳 Cooking up a fire dinner
  •  ₿ Reading about the latest crypto news
  • 💤 Falling asleep on the couch...
